OVERVIEW

On 10 May 2021, violent clashes shook civil society in Israel-Palestine as protests and demonstrations taking place in Jerusalem spread to other, particularly mixed, cities across Israel. Triggered by the impending eviction of Palestinian families from their homes in the East Jerusalem neighborhood of Sheikh Jarrah/Shimon haTzadik to make way for Jewish settlers, the confrontations rapidly intensified with the involvement of Hamas and Islamic Jihad in the Gaza Strip. During the events known in Arabic as Unity Intifada and in Hebrew as May 2021 Events, serious, direct confrontations took place at the civilian level between Palestinian and Jewish Israelis. The eleven-day clashes finally ended on 21 May with a ceasefire between Israel and Hamas.

This archive aims at documenting these events through a structured timeline, historical background, and curated resources.

BACKGROUND: SHEIKH JARRAH

Ottoman & British Mandate Period

Sheikh Jarrah is located in East Jerusalem, north of the Old City. During the late Ottoman period and the British Mandate, the neighborhood develops as a residential area with religious, political, and strategic significance. Its location near the Old City makes it part of wider disputes over urban space, property, and political control in Jerusalem.

1948 War and Jordanian Administration

The 1948 Arab-Israeli War transforms the status of Jerusalem and deeply affects Sheikh Jarrah. East Jerusalem comes under Jordanian control, while many Palestinians lose homes elsewhere. In the 1950s, Palestinian refugee families are housed in Sheikh Jarrah through arrangements involving Jordan and UNRWA, making the neighborhood closely connected to the history of displacement.

1967, Occupation & Settlement Claims

After the 1967 war, Israel occupies and annexes East Jerusalem. Israeli settler organizations begin to pursue property claims in Sheikh Jarrah, arguing that Jewish owners held land there before 1948. Palestinian residents, however, face a legal system that does not equally allow them to reclaim property lost in West Jerusalem or elsewhere in 1948.

Sheikh Jarrah in the 2000s and 2010s

In the 2000s and 2010s, eviction cases and settler takeovers make Sheikh Jarrah a visible symbol of Palestinian dispossession in East Jerusalem. Palestinian families contest eviction orders in court, while activists, residents, and international observers frame the neighborhood as a key site of resistance against settlement expansion.

Spring 2021: Court Cases, Protests and Escalation

In spring 2021, pending eviction decisions against Palestinian families in Sheikh Jarrah trigger renewed protests. Demonstrations in the neighborhood coincide with Ramadan tensions around the Old City and the Al-Aqsa Mosque compound. Sheikh Jarrah becomes one of the immediate flashpoints that connects local housing disputes to the wider May 2021 escalation.
